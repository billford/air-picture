No flagged contacts, because there were no contacts to flag. Anomaly status: the anomaly is the nothingness itself. Make of that what you will.

PATTERN NOTES

There is no pattern to note, because pattern requires data, and data requires aircraft, and aircraft require someone up there to be flying. Today, no one obliged.

If this were a single slow day, it would be unremarkable. But against a backdrop of nearly 200 daily overflights, a complete zeroing-out warrants at least a raised eyebrow and a second cup of coffee. Possible explanations include a regional weather diversion event, a data feed interruption, or — and this office offers this only as a theoretical possibility — every pilot in northeastern Ohio simply decided to take Sunday off on a Sunday that happens to fall on a Monday.

We will monitor.
